about

Eroica was born in London and grew up in Africa, specifically Cameroon, Libya and Senegal, and has also travelled extensively outwards from those countries to other parts of the continent as well. She received her education at various International Schools and at certain points in her life she has needed to speak Pidgin English, (Cameroon) Arabic and Italian (Libya) and Wolof and French (Senegal) just to make friends! She returned to the UK with a generic, but noticeable ‘African’ lilt, and there is such a thing! However, only French has remained in her system into adulthood as a regularly used language. Her enthusiasm for travelling, adventure and other cultures has taken her to many other world destinations since then, and she has also done ‘the big road trip’ across America, zigzagging from coast to coast, out of Los Angeles, ultimate destination New York.

After returning to the UK from Africa in her teens, Eroica attended Cheltenham Art College, always to be found in the sculpture studios. After this, she disappeared off to live the island life, in the lighthouse on the Ile de Porquerolles, one of four Mediterranean islands collectively called the Iles d'Hyères, located off the Cote d’Azur in the South of France. Returning to London, she became a fashion model by happenstance, after being spotted in a street off Berkley Square, going onto work in London, Paris, Milan, Hamburg and Munich – both on the catwalk, and for magazine photo- shoots. She lived full-time in both Milan and Paris during this period. Eroica then started up as a fashion stylist based in London, with an extensive client base both at home and abroad.

She moved over to writing after that, attending the University of East Anglia (UEA) as a mature student on the BA (Hons.) English Literature with Creative Writing Degree Course. The UEA is widely seen as ‘the home of creative writing’ due to its predominant role in pioneering this course in the UK back in the 1960’s. Eroica’s first novel had already published prior to attending the UEA, but it was nevertheless an invaluable clarifying experience going forward.

background

columnist

Eroica Mildmay has had column for eighteen months for the                       called Notes from a Norfolk Broad, which was a humorously rumbustious and much-read countryside- based column with a dedicated readership both here in the UK and around the world.

features writer / journalist

Eroica Mildmay worked for North Norfolk LIVING magazine for five years covering all sorts of subjects, interviewing, often actually taking part (as in microlight flying, canoeing, wild swimming, deep sea trawling for lobsters, moth hunting (for research purposes), driving a Royal National Lifeboat Institution (RNLI) rescue vessel on training exercises (specifically the extremely fast B Class Atlantic 75) and many other adventures. She did all the photography for the articles and obviously wrote the finished product. She had an extensive and dedicated readership in Norfolk and beyond.

novelist

Eroica Mildmay has published two novels. Her first Lucker and Tiffany Peel Out came out to excellent critical reviews in most of the leading broadsheets, tabloid newspapers and magazines here in the UK. Lucker and Tiffany Peel Out was also translated into several different languages for the foreign market and optioned for potential film production. Her second novella Home to Angel Cake also received only positive reviews. She has published a selection of short stories, including in the sci-fi genre, many of which have also been translated into foreign languages as well.

Eroica has just completed a third novel, The Day Gone.

freelance copywriter

Eroica Mildmay is a professional writer, experienced in several different genres, having worked as a copywriter, a features writer, as a journalist and in broadcast comedy. She is also a published novelist and short story writer. Eroica has a fresh, original and imaginatively creative approach to copy, and her manner of delivery and thorough writing style is crisp, punchy and incisive. Informative and inventively descriptive, her writing skills and experience lend verve and clarity to even the most basic of briefs.

what’s wanted?

Bang-up-to-date cutting-edge copy? Or perhaps a more traditional and classic style of prose is required? Or crisp formality, known formulas, brochure-speak, clean and clear instruction manuals, general blurb or perhaps even some historical context? Need some absolutely plain-speaking no- frills everyday copy? Or, a poetic approach perhaps, and by the way, poetical structuring comes easily to Eroica. Ghostwriting . . . woohoooo, let’s go! A regular blog maybe? Social media input and output? Engaging website content to go? Film scripts, no problem? Or, if needed, a comedic twist (as Eroica is in possession of a sharp wit!) So, if original thinking, one-off inventiveness, character-driven ideas or gentle humorous asides are required, or whatever, it’s all very achievable!

can do this!

Responsive and communicative, and with communication always being the absolute key, Eroica is able to work from even the flimsiest of concepts and enhance and flesh out any core idea into startling and attention-grabbing copy to suit. Good observational skills and picking up the idea immediately is essential in this line of work, as are excellent on-board proof-reading skills and attention to the overall presentation.

Eroica’s work is always highly finished and delivered in a timely fashion, as required. The challenge is the fun part and the satisfaction always comes in doing an outstanding job to a very high standard.
